# Community Content Strategy Skill

## CORE PRINCIPLE: Community Over Audience

The X Factor separating Supreme/Nike/Cortez from everyone else is **community** — not product quality, design, or price.

**Hard rule:** You cannot build a community around a product. You can ONLY build it around a **Big Idea** (ideology, culture, or lifestyle). The product represents it — it is not it.

> Simon Sinek: "People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it."
> Applied: They don't buy the T-shirt. They buy the greater conceptual meaning the brand represents.

Limbic brain controls decisions — emotion fires before logic. A fan turns down a logically better competitor because of emotional loyalty.

---

## THE FAN FRAMEWORK

Every content decision must serve one of these three goals:

| Letter | Meaning | What content achieves it |
|--------|---------|--------------------------|
| **F** | **Faithful** | Customer turns down competitors — emotional brand lock-in |
| **A** | **Advocate** | Customer becomes walking billboard — shares, tags, word-of-mouth |
| **N** | **Nurtured** | Customer returns drop after drop — repeat buyer loyalty |

Business case: If every customer bought 2× instead of 1× → revenue doubles. Community is the mechanism.

## THE BIG IDEA — 5 DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS

Before any content brief, these must be answered (stored in brand_big_idea):

1. **What does your brand name mean?** (etymology, concept, aspiration)
2. **Who is the ONE person that best embodies your brand?** (specific — a friend, celebrity, or archetype. Not a demographic.)
3. **What about them is aligned with the brand?** (personality traits, values, beliefs, known behaviours)
4. **If that person and others gathered, what would they be doing?** (the unifying activity — this is your content scenario universe)
5. **What is the greater mission beyond the clothing?** (the ideology that outlasts any single product)

**Strength test:** If someone can identify your brand without seeing your logo or product — just from the content — your Big Idea is strong enough.

**Nike example:**
1. Greek goddess of victory — sporting challenge and overcoming
2. Michael Jordan — disciplined, love of sport, never-give-up attitude
3. Gets up early, persistence, does the work on hard days
4. Playing basketball, working out, competing together
5. Bring inspiration and innovation to every athlete in the world

## CONTENT VOLUME RULE

You cannot force virality. You control volume.

> Double content = double chances at a sale.

**6 techniques — more content, less time:**

**1. Creator Army** — Micro-influencers send clips back to you.
DM followers who already post lifestyle content in your niche.
Win-win message: "Hey, I love your content about [X]. I run [brand] focused on [mission]. I'd love to send free clothing in exchange for a couple of videos of you styling them. You can post them too."

**2. Repeatable Series** — 2-3 series that run forever. Never invent from scratch.
- Founder Vlog: packing orders, checking samples, behind-scenes prep (1-2×/week)
- Customer Spotlight: repost a customer wearing brand + ask 2 questions about their life as it relates to the brand message. Their answer = caption story.
- Community Event: turn a brand campaign into a literal gathering. Film it.

**3. Bulk Production** — One shooting day per week. Walk away with a week (or month) of content. Plan all references beforehand. 1-2 shots per video max. Same location, same people.

**4. Platform Focus** — Quit all platforms except ONE until mastered.
- High-price / community brand → Instagram
- Low-price / impulse product → TikTok

**5. Repurpose** — Same footage, different hook/text = new video to the algorithm. Re-edit clips in different order. Screenshots from campaign → carousel posts.

**6. Blend Into Life** — Film real moments that match brand lifestyle. Gym → mental health brand. Hiking → outdoors brand. Training session → running brand. No fake content days.

## 7 INSTAGRAM MISTAKES (NEVER-DO LIST)

1. **Only product posts** — builds brand on sand. Fix: always show people *living* the lifestyle while wearing the product. Message + product together in every post.
2. **Niche too broad** ("premium streetwear") — Fix: find top 3 customers' social accounts, spot common traits via AI pattern match, pick one specific niche.
3. **Theme page disconnect** (pure lifestyle content + disconnected product drops) — Fix: every post mixes value AND product. Never pure lifestyle without the garment.
4. **Only founder's face** — Fix: feature others. Post as a collab (appears on both accounts, doubles reach). Community = multiple faces, not a personal brand.
5. **Fashion film shot landscape / no hook** — Fix: film portrait. Start mid-scene. Split one long film into 3 separate videos.
6. **Low quality content** — Fix: natural light (window behind camera, never behind subject). Less is more — fewer fonts, colors, elements. Hierarchy — subject is always the most prominent thing.
7. **Poor product setup** — Fix: 3 L's before every shoot: **Lighting** (natural, no shadows on product), **Location** (background doesn't distract), **Layout** (product laid neatly, you'd be proud to post it).

## GROWTH CONTENT METHOD

Do NOT post product ads to grow. Post lifestyle/identity content.

**3-step event framework:**
1. Identify someone who embodies the brand (customer avatar or specific friend)
2. Ask what they do in their free time
3. Pick the activity most related to brand identity AND achievable on small budget → turn it into a literal event, film it

**Content research method:**
1. Search **parallel terms** (directly related: "running") + **adjacent terms** (related niche: "marathon training"). Never search "fashion" — makes you look like every other brand.
2. Press **Reels filter** on Instagram Explore (Reels reach cold audiences > followers)
3. Scroll with intention — note format: what does the video DO structurally? What's the hook mechanism?
4. Apply **Virgil Abloh 3% Rule** — change ONE element by ~3%. Same familiar format, fresh for your brand. Never copy wholesale.

**Trial Reels A/B testing:**
- Create 5-10 versions of the same video, changing only ONE element (spoken hook, opening frame, or duration)
- Post all trials simultaneously — only reaches non-followers, won't appear on grid
- After 3-5 days, pick winner to post publicly to grid
- Every video gets its best possible chance before the audience sees it

## COMEDY SHORT FILM FORMULA

Highest-performing format for clothing brands on Instagram Reels. Lifestyle-focused, highly exaggerated, ≤60 seconds. Acts as scroll-stopper + brand ad simultaneously.

**Step 1 — Clarify Branding**
What is the lifestyle, belief, or ideology behind the brand?
- Ideological: mental health brand — belief system
- Lifestyle: skateboarding brand — specific activity
- Hybrid: self-improvement — ideology + lifestyle traits

**Step 2 — Pick a Scenario (least obvious)**
> Football brand: don't pick "on the pitch" → pick "locker room before game" or "setting out shoes at home the night before"
Least obvious = most original story. Keep it accessible: no skyscrapers or locations you can't access.

**Step 3 — Create a Character**
Your target customer. Exaggerate their traits: extremely good, bad, or ordinary at something.
- Goals must align with brand identity (winning brand → character's goal = win)
- Conflict must directly oppose the goal

**Step 4 — Infuse Conflict**
- Visually obvious (audience understands without narration)
- Comical, not serious — serious risks cringe. Exaggeration + irony = cool.
- Character juxtaposition — audience expects X, character is surprisingly Y

**Step 5 — Structure for Social Media**

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Hook | Start mid-scene. No orientation or character introductions. First frame = action or curiosity already happening. |
| Curiosity Gap | Raise questions without answering them (who? where? why?). Audience stays to find out. |
| Payoff | Unexpected twist in final 3-5 seconds. Rewards the viewer for watching. Makes them rewatch or share. |
| Tagline | 2-4 word brand punchline at end. Summarises entire ideology. e.g. "Take what's yours." |
| No-Sound Test | Watch without audio — story must still make sense. Nearly half of Instagram users watch silent. Zero or minimal dialogue preferred. |

## PLATFORM CONTENT RULES

| Platform | Caption length | Format | Primary goal |
|---------|---------------|--------|-------------|
| Instagram | 1-2 lines + hashtags | Portrait Reels, carousels | Loyal community, saves |
| Facebook | 3-5 lines + question CTA | Video, link posts | Community groups, shares |
| Threads | Under 280 chars | Raw thoughts, hot takes | Conversation, relatability |
| TikTok | 1 line | Vertical video | Discovery, impulse |

## CONTENT QUALITY FLOOR (mandatory checklist)

Before any content suggestion or brief is finalised:

- [ ] 3 L's passed — Lighting (natural), Location (non-distracting), Layout (product neat + intentional)
- [ ] No-sound test — story lands without audio
- [ ] FAN goal assigned — this piece serves Faithful / Advocate / or Nurtured
- [ ] Big Idea aligned — reinforces brand ideology, not just shows product
- [ ] System 1 score ≥ 3 — makes someone feel something in 2 seconds
- [ ] People in frame — at least one human wearing/using the product (no empty landscapes with quote overlays)
- [ ] Platform native — format matches the platform it's being posted to
