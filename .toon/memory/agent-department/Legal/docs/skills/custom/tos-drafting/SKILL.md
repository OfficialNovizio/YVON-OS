# Terms of Service Drafting

When this skill activates, draft legally structured Terms of Service. Cover standard sections required for enforceability across US, EU, and common law jurisdictions.

## Standard ToS Sections

### 1. Acceptance of Terms
- Effective date
- Binding agreement statement
- Age requirement (13+ COPPA, 16+ GDPR consent)
- Modification notice (30-day advance recommended)

### 2. Description of Service
- What the service is
- What it is NOT (no guarantees of specific outcomes)
- Service level expectations (if SLA exists, link separately)

### 3. User Accounts
- Registration requirements (accurate info)
- Account security responsibility
- Termination rights (both sides)
- Account deletion/data retention after termination

### 4. Payments and Billing
- Pricing disclosure
- Billing cycle (monthly/annual)
- Auto-renewal terms (must be explicit)
- Refund policy
- Tax responsibility
- Failed payment consequences

### 5. Intellectual Property
- Service IP: company owns the platform
- User IP: user owns their content
- License grant: user grants license to host/display
- Feedback license: any feedback becomes company property (standard but controversial)

### 6. User Conduct
- Prohibited uses (illegal, harmful, abusive)
- Content standards
- Enforcement rights (remove content, suspend accounts)

### 7. Third-Party Services
- Links to third parties not endorsed
- No liability for third-party content/actions

### 8. Disclaimers
- "AS IS" and "AS AVAILABLE"
- No warranty of merchantability
- No warranty of fitness for particular purpose
- No guarantee of uninterrupted service

### 9. Limitation of Liability
- Cap on damages (typically fees paid in last 12 months)
- Exclusion of indirect/consequential damages
- Exceptions for: death/personal injury, fraud, gross negligence (unenforceable to exclude)

### 10. Indemnification
- User indemnifies company for third-party claims arising from user's use
- Exceptions for company's own negligence

### 11. Dispute Resolution
- Governing law (Delaware common for US companies)
- Arbitration clause (AAA or JAMS)
- Class action waiver
- Venue for litigation
- Small claims court carve-out

### 12. Miscellaneous
- Severability
- Entire agreement
- Waiver
- Assignment
- Force majeure
- Notices

## Jurisdiction Considerations
- **US:** Delaware governing law, arbitration (FAA preemption)
- **EU:** Consumer protection laws override some limitations, GDPR references required
- **India:** IT Act 2000 compliance, grievance officer required

## Key Enforceability Rules
- Conspicuous disclaimers (ALL CAPS or bold for limitation of liability)
- Browsewrap vs clickwrap: clickwrap (I Agree button) is enforceable, browsewrap often isn't
- Unilateral modification: must notify users, give opt-out window
